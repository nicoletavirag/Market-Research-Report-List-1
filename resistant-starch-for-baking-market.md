<p><p>Resistant starch, a type of starch that is not readily digested in the small intestine, can be classified into four market types: RS2, RS1, RS3, and RS4. RS2 refers to starch that contains crystalline structures and is found in raw potatoes and green bananas. RS1 is the starch naturally present in grains, seeds, and legumes with a resistant structure. RS3 is created through the cooking and cooling process, such as in pasta or potatoes. RS4 is chemically modified to resist digestion, commonly used in food processing and baking products.</p></p>
