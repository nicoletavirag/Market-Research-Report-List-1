<p><p>Ammonium ion selective electrodes are devices used for measuring the concentration of ammonium ions in a solution. There are two main types of these electrodes: liquid membrane and PVC membrane. Liquid membrane electrodes consist of an organic liquid that selectively reacts with ammonium ions and generates a measurable electrical potential. PVC membrane electrodes, on the other hand, use a polyvinyl chloride (PVC) membrane containing an ammonium-selective ionophore. Both types of electrodes are widely used in various industries such as environmental analysis, agriculture, and wastewater treatment.</p></p>
