<p><p>Embolization is a minimally invasive procedure used in interventional oncology to selectively block blood flow to tumors or abnormal blood vessels. The increasing prevalence of cancer, advancements in minimally invasive treatments, and a growing preference for targeted therapy are driving the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising adoption of embolization by healthcare professionals as it provides a safe and effective alternative to surgical interventions is also contributing to market expansion. Embolization procedures are less invasive, offer quicker recovery times, and have lower risks compared to surgery, making them attractive to both patients and healthcare providers.</p><p>In addition, advancements in embolization technologies such as improved imaging guidance systems, targeted embolic agents, and combination therapies are enhancing the effectiveness of the procedure, further boosting market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. Combining embolization with these therapies enhances their effectiveness and improves patient outcomes.</p><p>Another trend is the growing use of radioembolization, a type of embolization that involves the use of radioactive substances to deliver targeted radiation to tumors.
