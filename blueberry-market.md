<p><p>Blueberry market types refer to different varieties of blueberries that are grown for commercial purposes. Highbush blueberries are the most common type, cultivated in large quantities for fresh consumption and processing. Lowbush blueberries are wild varieties that are harvested from natural stands and used mainly for processing into jams and jellies. Hybrid half-high blueberries are a cross between highbush and lowbush varieties, combining the best traits of both. Rabbiteye blueberries are grown in warmer regions, known for their resistance to certain diseases and suitability for harvesting mechanically.</p></p>
