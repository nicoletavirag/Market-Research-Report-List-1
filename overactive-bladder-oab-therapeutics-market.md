<p><p>Overactive bladder (OAB) therapeutics market includes different types of medications for managing the condition. Anticholinergic agents are commonly prescribed drugs that help relax the bladder muscles, reducing involuntary contractions. They work by blocking the action of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates bladder contractions. Beta-3 adrenoreceptor agonists are another type of OAB medication that work by stimulating specific receptors in the bladder, promoting relaxation of the bladder muscle and increasing bladder capacity. Both these types of medications aim to improve symptoms like frequent urination, urgency, and urinary incontinence associated with overactive bladder.</p></p>
