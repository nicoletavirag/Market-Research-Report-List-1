<p><p>The mortar fuzes market consists of two types: mechanical and electronic. Mechanical fuzes are simple mechanisms that rely on mechanical forces, such as impact or time delays, to initiate the explosion of a mortar round. They are reliable and cost-effective but lack advanced features. On the other hand, electronic fuzes incorporate advanced technology like microprocessors and sensors to provide increased precision and safety. They offer features such as programmable delays and self-destruct options, enhancing their effectiveness in modern warfare scenarios.</p></p>
