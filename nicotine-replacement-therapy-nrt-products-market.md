<p><p>Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT) products come in various types to help people quit smoking. Sprays deliver a fine mist of nicotine into the mouth, while inhalers release nicotine vapor for inhalation. Chewing gums contain nicotine that is slowly released when chewed. Transdermal patches are applied to the skin, gradually releasing nicotine into the body. Other NRT options could include lozenges, mouth sprays, or even electronic cigarettes. These different products aim to provide smokers with a controlled dose of nicotine to help alleviate withdrawal symptoms and gradually reduce dependency on tobacco.</p></p>
