<p><p>Cochlear implants are electronic devices that provide a sense of sound to individuals with severe to profound hearing loss. There are two main types of cochlear implants in the market: unilateral and binaural. Unilateral implants are designed to be implanted in one ear, providing hearing abilities to that specific side. On the other hand, binaural implants are meant to be implanted in both ears, offering sound perception from both sides. Both types aim to improve hearing ability and enhance the overall quality of life for individuals with hearing impairments.</p></p>
