<p><p>A pancreatic and biliary stent is a medical device used to treat blockage or narrowing of the pancreatic or biliary duct. It is inserted into the duct to maintain its patency, allowing the passage of fluids and relieving symptoms associated with the obstruction. These stents are commonly made of plastic or metal, and they can be temporary or permanent depending on the condition being treated.</p><p>The global pancreatic and biliary stent market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 7.3% during the forecast period. There are several factors contributing to this growth. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of pancreatic and biliary disorders, such as pancreatic cancer, gallstones, and biliary strictures, is driving the demand for stent placement procedures. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more susceptible to these conditions, is further fueling market growth.</p><p>Technological advancements in stent design and materials are also driving market development. The development of fully covered self-expanding metal stents (FCSEMS) has significantly improved patient outcomes by reducing stent-related complications. Furthermore, the introduction of biodegradable stents and drug-eluting stents has opened up new avenues for the treatment of pancreatic and biliary diseases.</p><p>In terms of geographical segmentation, North America dominates the market, primarily due to the high prevalence of pancreatic and biliary disorders in the region and the presence of well-established healthcare infrastructure. However, the Asia-Pacific region is expected to witness the highest growth rate during the forecast period, attributed to increasing healthcare expenditure, improving healthcare facilities, and rising awareness about advanced treatment options.</p><p>Overall, the pancreatic and biliary stent market is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as the growing prevalence of pancreatic and biliary disorders, technological advancements, and increasing healthcare expenditure.</p></p>

<p><p>The pancreatic and biliary stent market consists of two types: plastic and metal stents. Plastic stents are made of a flexible material and are commonly used for temporary relief of obstructions in the pancreatic and biliary ducts. They are easily removable but may need to be replaced more frequently. Metal stents, on the other hand, are made of a stainless steel or nitinol alloy and are typically used for long-term management of obstructions. These stents offer better radial force and durability, making them less likely to migrate or block the ducts.</p></p>
