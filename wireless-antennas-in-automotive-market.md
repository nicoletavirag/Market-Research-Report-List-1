<p><p>Wireless antennas are widely used in the automotive market for various purposes. UHF (Ultra High Frequency) antennas are designed to operate on frequencies above 300 MHz, providing a longer range and better penetration through obstacles. They are commonly used for applications such as remote keyless entry systems and tire pressure monitoring systems. VHF (Very High Frequency) antennas, on the other hand, operate on frequencies between 30 and 300 MHz and are often used for applications like car radios. Both UHF and VHF antennas play a crucial role in ensuring reliable wireless communication within vehicles.</p></p>
