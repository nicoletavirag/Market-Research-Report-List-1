<p><p>An interlocking intramedullary nail is a medical device used in orthopedic surgery to treat long bone fractures. It is a metal rod inserted into the medullary canal of the bone to stabilize the fracture and promote healing. The nail is secured with interlocking screws at both ends of the fracture site, providing stability and allowing early mobilization of the patient.</p><p>The global interlocking intramedullary nail market is projected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. The market growth is driven by factors such as the rising incidence of long bone fractures, increasing geriatric population, advancements in implant materials and design, and the growing demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. One such trend is the development of biodegradable interlocking intramedullary nails. These nails are made from bioresorbable materials that gradually degrade over time, eliminating the need for a second surgery to remove the implant.</p><p>Another trend in the interlocking intramedullary nail market is the adoption of computer-assisted surgery (CAS) techniques. CAS uses computer imaging and navigation systems to improve the accuracy and precision of nail insertion, leading to better patient outcomes.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a surge in research and development activities aimed at developing innovative implant designs and materials. These advancements aim to enhance the strength, flexibility, and biocompatibility of interlocking intramedullary nails.</p><p>In conclusion, the interlocking intramedullary nail market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.5% during the forecast period.
