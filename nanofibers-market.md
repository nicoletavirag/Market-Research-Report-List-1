<p><p>Nanofibers are ultrafine fibers with diameters in the nanometer scale. The market for nanofibers can be classified into various types. Polymer nanofibers are made from synthetic or natural polymers and find applications in filtration and tissue engineering. Carbon nanofibers are composed of carbon atoms and are used in aerospace and automotive industries. Glass nanofibers have high tensile strength and are used in insulation and filtration. Ceramic nanofibers possess high heat resistance and find applications in catalysis and energy storage. Composite nanofibers combine different materials, offering enhanced properties. Metallic nanofibers are made of metals such as silver or gold, and are used in electronics and sensors.</p></p>
