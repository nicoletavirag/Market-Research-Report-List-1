<p><p>Factor IX deficiency treatment refers to the management of hemophilia B, a genetic disorder characterized by the deficiency or dysfunction of clotting factor IX. The treatment primarily aims to control bleeding episodes and prevent complications such as joint damage, while improving the quality of life for patients.</p><p>The main treatment options for factor IX deficiency include replacement therapy and gene therapy. Replacement therapy involves the intravenous administration of factor IX concentrate to replace the deficient clotting factor. This can be administered as prophylaxis (regular infusions to prevent bleeding) or on-demand (infusions to treat active bleeding). Gene therapy, on the other hand, involves the delivery of functional factor IX genes to the patient's liver cells, enabling their bodies to produce the clotting factor naturally.</p><p>The factor IX deficiency treatment market is expected to have significant growth in the coming years.
