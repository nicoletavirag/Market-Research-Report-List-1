<p><p>Drug and gene delivery devices refer to the various methods used to administer medications or genes into the body. The market types include oral, inhalation, transdermal, injectable, ocular, nasal, and topical delivery systems. Oral delivery involves drugs being ingested through the mouth and absorbed in the digestive system. Inhalation delivery involves inhaling drugs in the form of gases or aerosols. Transdermal delivery involves applying drugs onto the skin for absorption. Injectable delivery involves injecting drugs directly into the body through a syringe or needle. Ocular delivery involves delivering drugs to the eyes. Nasal delivery involves administering drugs through the nasal cavity. Topical delivery involves applying drugs directly on the surface of the skin.</p></p>
