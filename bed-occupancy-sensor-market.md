<p><p>A bed occupancy sensor is a device that is used to detect the presence or absence of a person on a bed. It is typically used in healthcare settings such as hospitals, nursing homes, and assisted living facilities to monitor the activity and movement of patients or residents. The sensor is usually placed under the mattress or embedded within the bed frame and it uses various technologies such as pressure sensing, infrared sensing, or motion sensing to detect when a person is lying or sitting on the bed.</p><p>The bed occupancy sensor market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for advanced healthcare monitoring and management systems. This technology offers several benefits such as improved patient safety, reduced fall rates, efficient bed management, and enhanced workflow in healthcare facilities. The market is also driven by the rising geriatric population, the growing prevalence of chronic diseases, and the need for cost-effective healthcare solutions.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. For instance, there is a growing focus on the development of wireless and battery-powered bed occupancy sensors, which offer ease of installation and increased flexibility in terms of bed placement.
