<p><p>Silicon-free Thermal Interface Material (TIM) is a type of material used for enhancing thermal conductivity and heat dissipation between two surfaces in electronic devices. The increasing demand for enhanced thermal management solutions in electronic devices is a major driver for market growth. As electronic devices continue to become smaller and more powerful, the need for efficient cooling systems has become critical to prevent overheating.</p><p>Moreover, the growing adoption of electric vehicles (EVs) and the increasing focus on energy efficiency in industries such as telecommunications, consumer electronics, and aerospace are driving the demand for silicon-free TIMs. These materials offer improved thermal conductivity, stability, and reliability in demanding environments.</p><p>In terms of trends, there has been a rising demand for phase change materials (PCMs) as a silicon-free alternative in TIMs. PCMs have the ability to absorb and release heat through phase transitions, making them ideal for high-performance applications.
