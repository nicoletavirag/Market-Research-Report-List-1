<p><p>Position and proximity sensors are crucial components in various industries for detecting the position or presence of objects or people. Contact sensors directly come into physical contact with the object of interest, such as limit switches or touch sensors, providing accurate measurements. On the other hand, non-contact sensors, including optical, ultrasonic, and magnetic sensors, detect positions or proximity without physical contact. Non-contact sensors offer advantages like longer lifespans, less wear and tear, and better reliability, making them suitable for applications with delicate or fast-moving objects.</p></p>
