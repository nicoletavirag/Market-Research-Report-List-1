<p><h1>Contact Steering Torque Sensor Market Growth, Market Trends, COVID-19 Impact, and Forecasts for period from 2024 - 2031</h1></p><p><strong>Contact Steering Torque Sensor Market Analysis and Latest Trends</strong></p>
<p><p>A contact steering torque sensor is a device that measures the torque applied to the steering wheel in a vehicle. It is designed to provide accurate and reliable measurements of the driver's steering input. The sensor typically consists of a torsion bar that absorbs the steering torque and a strain gauge that converts the torque into an electrical signal.</p><p>The contact steering torque sensor market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and autonomous vehicles is one of the key factors driving this growth. As these technologies become more prevalent, the need for precise steering torque measurement will continue to increase.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing implementation of electric power steering (EPS) systems in vehicles is also contributing to the market growth. EPS systems rely on accurate torque measurements for their operation, and therefore, the demand for contact steering torque sensors is expected to rise.</p><p>In addition to these factors, the contact steering torque sensor market is also driven by the increasing penetration of electric and hybrid vehicles. These vehicles often use EPS systems and require accurate steering torque measurement for their efficient operation.</p><p>Regarding the latest trends in the contact steering torque sensor market, there is a growing focus on the development of non-contact or contactless steering torque sensors. These sensors eliminate the need for physical contact between the torsion bar and strain gauge, resulting in improved durability and reliability.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in sensor technologies, such as the integration of microelectromechanical systems (MEMS), are also expected to drive market growth. MEMS-based sensors offer enhanced accuracy and responsiveness, making them suitable for advanced steering systems.</p><p>Overall, the contact steering torque sensor market is poised for significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for ADAS, autonomous vehicles, electric power steering systems, and the emergence of non-contact sensor technologies. The market is anticipated to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 5% during the forecast period.</p></p>
