<p><p>Line-handling boats are designed specifically to handle lines and perform various tasks such as towing, docking, and mooring. Inboard motor line-handling boats are equipped with an engine mounted inside the hull, offering better weight distribution and increased maneuverability. On the other hand, outboard motor line-handling boats have the engine mounted externally on the transom, providing easy maintenance access and superb acceleration. Both types cater to different market preferences, with inboard motors being favored for performance and maneuverability, while outboard motors are popular for their simplicity and convenience.</p></p>
