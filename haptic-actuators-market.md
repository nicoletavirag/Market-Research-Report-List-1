<p><p>Haptic actuators are devices that generate tactile sensations to enhance user experience in electronic devices. Eccentric Rotating Mass (ERM) actuators create vibrations by unbalancing a rotating mass, commonly used in older devices. Linear Resonant Actuators (LRAs) generate haptic feedback through a spring-mass system, producing precise vibrations and stronger force control. Other types of haptic actuators include Piezoelectric actuators, Ultrasonic actuators, and Electroactive polymers, which induce vibrations using different mechanisms. All these actuators are employed in various electronic devices like smartphones, gaming consoles, and wearables to provide users with realistic touch feedback.</p></p>
