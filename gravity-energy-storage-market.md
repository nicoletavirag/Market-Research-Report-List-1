<p><p>Gravity energy storage is a form of energy storage that utilizes gravitational potential energy to store and release electricity. It involves lifting heavy weights to a higher elevation when excess electricity is available and then allowing them to drop to a lower elevation when the electricity is needed. The potential energy accumulated is then converted back to electricity.</p><p>One of the key advantages of gravity energy storage is its ability to provide large-scale, long-duration energy storage, offering grid stability and enabling the integration of high levels of renewable energy sources. It can store large amounts of electricity for hours or even days, ensuring a reliable and stable energy supply even during periods of intermittent generation.</p><p>The global gravity energy storage market is experiencing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding at a CAGR of 12.5% during the forecast period.
