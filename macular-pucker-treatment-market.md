<p><p>Macular pucker treatment refers to the medical interventions used to address macular pucker, also known as epiretinal membrane or cellophane maculopathy. Macular pucker occurs when a thin layer of scar tissue forms on the surface of the retina, leading to wrinkling or bulging of the macula. This condition can cause blurry or distorted vision, as well as decreased visual acuity.</p><p>The treatment of macular pucker may include medication, vitrectomy surgery, or less invasive procedures such as laser therapy or injection of medication into the eye.
