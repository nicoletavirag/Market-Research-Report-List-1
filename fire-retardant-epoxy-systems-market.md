<p><ul><li>Halogenated Fire Retardant</li><li>Inorganic Fire Retardant</li><li>Organic Phosphorus Fire Retardant</li><li>Nitrogenous Fire Retardant</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fire retardant epoxy systems market is categorized into four types: halogenated fire retardant, inorganic fire retardant, organic phosphorus fire retardant, and nitrogenous fire retardant. Halogenated fire retardants contain halogens like chlorine or bromine that release poisonous gases when exposed to fire. Inorganic fire retardants use materials like aluminum trihydrate to release water vapor when heated, suppressing flames. Organic phosphorus fire retardants inhibit the combustion process by releasing phosphorous radicals. Nitrogenous fire retardants work by releasing non-flammable gases like nitrogen or ammonia when heated.</p></p>
