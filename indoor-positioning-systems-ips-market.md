<p><p>Indoor Positioning Systems (IPS) utilize various technologies to determine the location of objects or people inside a building. Radio waves-based IPS use Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or RFID signals for positioning. Magnetic fields-based IPS rely on detecting anomalies in the Earth's magnetic field. Acoustic signal-based IPS employ sound waves for localization. Other types of IPS may involve infrared, visual light, ultrasonic waves, or even sensors embedded in floors. These technologies allow for precise indoor positioning, enabling applications such as indoor navigation, asset tracking, and location-based services.</p></p>
