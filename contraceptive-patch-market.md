<p><p>A contraceptive patch is a birth control method that involves the use of a small, patch-like device that is applied to the skin. The patch releases hormones (usually a combination of estrogen and progestin) into the body to prevent pregnancy. It is typically worn on the lower abdomen, buttocks, or upper body, and needs to be replaced once a week.</p><p>The contraceptive patch market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue to grow at a CAGR of 6.9% during the forecast period. The increasing popularity of the contraceptive patch can be attributed to its convenience, effectiveness, and ease of use. Unlike other forms of contraception, the patch does not require daily administration or insertion and has a high efficacy rate in preventing unwanted pregnancies.</p><p>Additionally, the contraceptive patch offers several benefits such as shorter, lighter, and less painful periods, as well as reducing the risk of certain health conditions like ovarian and endometrial cancer. These advantages have contributed to the growing adoption of the contraceptive patch among women.</p><p>The market for contraceptive patches has also been driven by the rise in awareness about family planning and the importance of birth control.
