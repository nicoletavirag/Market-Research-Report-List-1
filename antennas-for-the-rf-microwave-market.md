<p><p>Omni-directional antennas are designed to radiate signals uniformly in all directions, providing 360-degree coverage. They are widely used in applications where signals need to be transmitted or received from multiple directions simultaneously, such as wireless routers and mobile devices. </p><p>On the other hand, directional antennas focus signal transmission or reception in a specific direction, providing long-range coverage and higher gain. They are commonly used in point-to-point communication scenarios, like satellite dishes, and applications where signals need to be concentrated towards a particular area, such as in wireless backhaul systems. These antennas enable improved signal strength and reduced interference in targeted directions.</p></p>
